The University of Oxford is a world-leading center of learning, teaching, and research. It holds the distinction of being the oldest university in the English-speaking world, with a continuous existence spanning nine centuries.

History and Legacy:

Founded in 1096, the University of Oxford has a rich history and has played a pivotal role in shaping education, scholarship, and intellectual pursuits.

It is the second-oldest university in continuous operation globally

Global Reputation:

The Times Higher Education World University Rankings consistently place Oxford among the top five universities worldwide.

Its campus is home to approximately 26,000 students, including a significant international community representing over 160 countries.

Economic Impact:

A study has measured Oxford University’s impact on the UK economy, highlighting its significant contributions to research, innovation, and education.
